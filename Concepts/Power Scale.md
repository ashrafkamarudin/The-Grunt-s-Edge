# ⚔️ POWER STRUCTURE — The Grunt's Edge World

These are levels of individual and group combat strength, not social positions. Outliers exist, but this is the general framework.

## Important: Tiers Are Not Absolute

These tiers describe general combat strength, not guaranteed outcomes. A Tier 5 can still be killed by a Tier 3 — through underhanded tactics, ambushes, numbers (hundreds against one), terrain advantage, or exploiting exhaustion and mana depletion.

Tiers measure capability, not inevitability. Strategy can overcome raw power.

---

# 🪖 TIER 0 — CIVILIANS (NON-COMBATANTS)

## Who they are
- Farmers, merchants, craftsmen, porters  
- No combat training  
- No access to magic systems  

## Mana
- Most have no mana cores
- Some have Level 1 cores — enough for utility, not combat

## Narrative role
> The people Eric is ultimately trying to protect (even if he doesn't admit it)

---

# 🪓 TIER 1 — FOOT SOLDIERS (ERIC'S LEVEL)

## Who they are
- Infantry, spearmen, shield units  
- Basic trained recruits  
- Some veterans  

## Capability
- Can kill monsters only in groups  
- Rely on formation > individual skill  
- Survival depends on discipline, not talent  

## Mana
- Most have no cores or Level 1-2

## Reality check
- Most deaths happen here  
- Individual strength barely matters  

## Strength definition
> "Can you survive a battle line collapse?"

Eric sits here.

---

# 🧱 TIER 2 — ELITE SOLDIERS / SPECIALISTS

## Who they are
- Veteran infantry  
- Monster hunters  
- Knight trainees  
- Mage trainees  

## Capability
- Can handle monsters solo in favorable conditions  
- Fight in small groups instead of full formations  
- Tactical awareness > raw strength  

## Mana
- Knight trainees: Level 2-3 cores
- Mage trainees: Level 3 minimum

## Role in war
- Turn chaotic fights into manageable ones  
- Anchor battlefield stability  

> This is where "named soldiers" begin to exist

Eric will brush against this tier over time.

---

# 🛡️ TIER 3 — KNIGHTS AND MAGES

## Who they are
- Fully realized knights (graduated, combat-ready)
- Fully realized mages (graduated, certified)
- Research mages at Level 4

## Capability
- Knights: burst aura — momentary advantages (speed, blade reinforcement, impact hardening, air shield). This is achieved through Spellforms — the knight triggers a pre-etched pattern rather than shaping mana freely.
- Knights without Control talent rely on Spellforms for their abilities. A knight's combat capability depends on which Spellforms they have etched and their mana capacity to power them.
- Not all knights use aura — some reach this tier through pure martial skill
- Mages: external projection — elemental, spatial, mental spells

## Mana
- Knights typically range from Level 3-5
- Knights without mana cores exist — they reach this tier through combat skill alone
- Mages require Level 4 minimum — most are Level 5-6
- Level 3 is the minimum threshold for knight training

## Important limitation
> This is the first tier where individual combat power becomes meaningful.
> Still limited by stamina, mana supply, and battlefield conditions.

---

# ⚔️ TIER 4 — HEROES

## Who they are
- Recognized individuals from any class
- Set apart by reputation, experience, and proven performance under extreme conditions

## Capability
- Hero-knights, hero-mages, hero-healers — all are heroes
- Still bound by the same mana system and physical limits as their class

## Mana
- Any level — heroes are defined by reputation and performance, not core capacity

## But important limitation:
> They are NOT independent war winners.

They are:
- force multipliers  
- not armies  

Without logistics + infantry support:
- they die or retreat  

## Social perception vs reality

### Public view:
> Heroes win wars

### Military reality:
> Heroes survive battles long enough for armies to win wars

---

# 🔥 TIER 5 — ARTILLERY CLASS

Not an individual. A technique requiring multiple participants.

## How it works
- One chanter (must be a mage, Level 6-7) shapes the spell
- Multiple mana suppliers (10s, 50s, or even hundreds) feed their mana into the chanter
- The resulting spell is far beyond what any single mage could cast

## Participants
- Mana suppliers can be any level — even Level 1-2 cores can contribute

## Limitations
- Requires coordination, preparation, and protection
- Extremely vulnerable to disruption
- Participants are immobile or slowed during the process
- Used for battlefield-scale destruction, terrain alteration, anti-monster operations

# 🏛️ ORGANIZATIONAL POWER (APPENDIX — NOT COMBAT TIERS)

The following influence battles, but they are not strength tiers. They are systems and positions.

## Command-Level
Generals, high nobles, arch-mages. They do not fight directly. They control outcomes through troop movement, supply chains, battlefield positioning, and deployment of heroes and mages.

> They decide who wins before the battle even starts.

## System Dominance
Logistics networks, supply chains, recruitment systems, military doctrine, intelligence networks.

> Armies do not win because of heroes. They win because systems don't collapse.

If systems break:
- heroes die  
- mages run out of mana supplies  
- infantry collapses
