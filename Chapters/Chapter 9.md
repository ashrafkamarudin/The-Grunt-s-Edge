# Chapter 9 — The Line

I wake to movement.

The tent is still dark but the veterans are already up — not ordered, not rushed, just moving. Sutter is stretching his arms. Mila is lacing her boots. Renn is already outside.

I sit up. Darrin stirs beside me.

Sutter sees us. *"We're going for morning training. Come join us."*

We follow.

---

There's an open patch near the tents, that's where the veterans gather and trains. They do basic formation, shield positioning, spear thrusts. The three of us newbies stand at the edge and watch.

While observing, even without much experience myself, I notice the difference right away. Their movements are nothing like the conscripts at Grayford. They shift their feet before the command is finished. Shields rose together. Feet shifted together. Spears came forward in the same rhythm.

Nobody looked down to check their grip.

Nobody hesitated.

They just did it. *'...they're not thinking about it anymore.'*

There I notice 2 unfamiliar faces.. Should be Gren and Larris who returned from medical tent.

Gren's arm is wrapped tighter than before, movement slightly stiff at the shoulder. Larris looks worse — still quiet, but now with a faint limp that doesn't quite disappear when he walks.

No one announces them. They just… reappear in formation like they were always part of it.

Gren notices my glance and gives a short nod.

Not friendly. Not cold either.

Corbin moves through them, sometimes facilitating, sometimes joining in. He doesn't look impressive. That's what stands out. The officer at Grayford wanted to look strong. Corbin just looks like a man doing a job he's done too many times. 

Then the formation shifts. A command is given. Every shield locks together. The line becomes a wall. For a moment, I can almost imagine something slamming into it from the other side. Nobody moves. Nobody breaks.

*'...oh.'*

Suddenly the drills from Grayford don't seem quite as pointless.

Wells mutters beside me. Not loud, but enough.

*"Do we... have to train even here? I don't want to."*

Corbin hears him. He doesn't stop what he's doing. He just says, *"Out there, you won’t have time to think. Your body moves first. You’ll just do what you’ve done a hundred times… and that might be what keeps you alive"*

Wells looks away. Nobody argues. The veterans reset for another round.

I watch them for another few seconds. Then I step forward and pick up the shield and spear too.

Darrin follows immediately.

Wells hesitates. He looks at us. He looks at the veterans. Then lets out a quiet curse and follows too.

We join the formation and drill with the squad for the rest of the morning.

---

After squad training, the veterans disperse. Some rest. Some sharpen weapons. Some work alone like the others don’t exist.

Wells disappears without a word — not hiding, just choosing distance where no one expects anything from him.

I find a patch of open ground and start practicing. Spear thrusts. Footwork. Reset. Again. Nothing fancy. Just repetition until my body stops feeling like it’s borrowing movements from someone else.

At some point, I pause.

Corbin is a few paces away and he’s training alone.

I don’t mean “alone” like no one is with him. I mean it like no one belongs in the same rhythm as him. His spear moves without hesitation. Not fast. Not showy. Just… certain. Every step arrives before the decision. Every strike ends exactly where it should. There’s no wasted motion. No correction mid-movement. It doesn’t look like training. It looks like something that survived too many fights to be wrong anymore.

I realize I’ve been watching for too long when suddenly Darrin walks past me and stops in front of him.

*"Train me."*

Corbin doesn’t stop moving. *"No."*

No irritation. No interest. Just refusal.

Darrin stares for a second longer, then walks away like the answer was expected.

The next day, he tries again.

*"Train me."*

Same answer. *"No."*

That’s it. No explanation. No change in tone.

---

That evening, Sutter speaks while eating. Not to anyone in particular.

"Might not be a bad idea," he says, chewing slowly.
"Give the newbies a better chance."

No one responds. Corbin doesn't even look up, he just.. continues eating.

The following morning, after squad training, Corbin finally stops Darrin. He studies him for a moment.

Then, *"Fine."*

A pause.

*"Don’t waste my time."*

Darrin nods once. That’s enough.

I step forward.

*"Can I join too?"*

Corbin glances at me briefly. *"Fine."* Then, after a beat: *"Same rule."*

He doesn't explain what the rule is. He doesn't need to.

Renn joins the next day without asking. Just picks up his spear and stands in line like he had always been part of it.

Corbin watches him once. Says nothing.

Training doesn't change after that. It just expands. Spears. Footwork. Shield angles. Repetition. Corbin doesn't teach like an instructor. He demonstrates once. Then walks the line. A hand adjusts a grip. A foot gets nudged half an inch back. A shoulder gets pushed down just enough to stop tension building wrong. No praise. No frustration. Only correction.

At one point, I push too hard. Then Darrin matches it. They don't talk about it — they just keep going until their arms start to shake.

The next round is worse. Breathing gets heavier. Movements start to lag. Corbin stops the formation. Not sharply. Just raises a hand. Everyone freezes. He looks at them for a moment. Then speaks.

*"That’s enough."*

A pause.

Then, quieter: *"Training when you’re exhausted doesn't build anything."*

He taps the shaft of his spear lightly against the ground.

*"It breaks what you already learned."*

He looks at Darrin. Then me. Then the rest.

*"Save something for tomorrow."*

A longer pause. Then, like it's just fact:

*"You don't get stronger by emptying yourself every day."*

He turns away.

*"You get stronger by still being able to move the next day."*

...

After that, the rhythm sets in. No one announces it. No one agrees to it.

It just becomes the shape of the days. Train. Eat. Rest. Train again. 

Corbin never pushes them past what they can recover from.
But he never lets them stop early either. And somewhere in that rhythm, I start to notice something. Not improvement. Not strength. Just the slow disappearance of hesitation in my own body.

---

The days settle into a rhythm - Squad training at dawn. Individual drills after. Meals in between. Rest wherever there’s time left. No one calls it routine. But it becomes one anyway.

Over meals, the tent slowly finds its voice. Renn is usually the first to speak. Not because he has anything important to say, but because silence makes everything worse.

*"So, new guy,"* he says one morning, pointing his spoon at Eric. *"You planning to survive longer than a week or are you just here to make the rest of us look better?"*

A few chuckles ripple through the tent.

I just look down at my bowl. *"Planning to survive."*

"Bold strategy," Renn nods solemnly. *"I respect it."*

Mila leans back on her elbows like she owns the place, grinning even with porridge on her lip. *"Ignore him,"* she says to Eric. *"He says that to everyone. I think he’s just jealous you’re younger."*

Renn gasps. *"I’m in my prime."*

*"You’re in your decline,"* Mila shoots back instantly.

That gets a real laugh from Sutter.

Then, Renn talks about Fallmoor. Not like it’s gone. Not like it’s precious. Like it’s just… inconveniently far away.

*"It’s way out past Grayford,"* he says one evening, pushing his food around.
*"So far out you start wondering if maps just give up halfway."*

He grins faintly.

*"Good place to be from, though. Less people telling you what to do."*

No one argues with that.

Mila snorts.

"Yeah, and fewer people to borrow money from."

That gets a couple quiet laughs.

Sutter doesn’t speak much, but when he does, it lands.

*"Farmoor’s got better food than this,"* he says, poking at the mess with his spoon.

Renn freezes. *"Why do you know that?"*

*"I passed through once,"* Sutter replies. *"Your bread was too honest."*

That becomes a running joke for days.

*"Farmoor bread hit harder than training,"* Mila repeats later, laughing.

Renn never lets it go.

*"I feel personally attacked by a man who doesn’t even live there anymore."*

Pell mostly listens. He doesn’t interrupt. But sometimes he adds a word at exactly the right time.

Someone complains about boots.

*"Sucks."*

Someone talks about sleep.

*"Rare."*

No one ever questions it. It fits him.

---

After a few afternoons of training, Renn steps back during a drill and watches Darrin complete a sequence.

*"You're catching on fast,"* he says. Not jealous — impressed. *"Probably better than me already. And I've been here two weeks before you."*

Darrin doesn't respond. Just resets his stance and goes again.

I watch. File it away. *'He's learning faster than I am.'*

---

The horn comes mid-morning.

Not the routine signal — different. Longer. Urgent. It rips through the air and hangs there like it refuses to leave.

For a moment, none of the newbies move.

Wells is staring at the tent wall like it might explain itself. Darrin has frozen mid-step, one hand still on his strap. I’m holding my spear, and I don’t remember picking it up.

*‘What—’*

Then the tent breaks.

Not slowly. Not orderly.

It snaps into motion.

The veterans are already moving — gear in hands, shields strapped, bodies slipping past us like we’re not even there yet. Gren almost knocks Wells flat reaching for his kit. Mila brushes past me and drives her boot into mine just enough to jolt me back into my body.

*"Move!"*

Corbin’s voice cuts through it all.

Not loud. Not commanding.

Just someone who’s already accepted what’s coming.

*"Armor. Shields. Now."*

We spill out into the fort.

The world outside is already on fire with movement.

Horns sound again — shorter this time. Two sharp blasts from different directions, overlapping like they’re answering each other. Somewhere beyond the walls, another horn replies late, like it’s already behind.

Voices stack over voices.

*"Left flank forming!"*

*"Second line, tighten up!"*

*"Where’s support unit four? I said FOUR!"*

A rider cuts through the yard, shouting without slowing down.

*"Move to wall sectors! Don’t clog the gate!"*

Someone else screams back, unintelligible except for anger.

Boots hammer stone. Metal clacks. Shields scrape as people try to form lines that don’t want to exist yet.

We’re pushed into the flow of it.

No one is guiding us gently anymore — just redirecting pressure.

A conscript in front of me is grabbed by the shoulder and shoved sideways into formation like a piece in the wrong place.

*"Left flank! LEFT FLANK!"*

Corbin moves with us as we run.

Not leading — just staying close enough that the new ones don’t drift.

His voice cuts through gaps between commands.

*"Forest Hounds. Pack units. Fast. They probe the line before committing."*

Another horn blasts — closer this time.

Someone nearby swears under their breath.

Corbin doesn’t look at them.

*"They will hit edges first. That’s where we are."*

We turn a corner into the forming line.

Ahead, shield walls are already assembling — uneven at first, then tightening as veterans correct spacing by force of habit alone.

A commander somewhere yells again:

*"Hold the flank! If it breaks, we’re gone!"*

No explanation of what “gone” means.

Everyone understands anyway.

Corbin keeps speaking as we slot into position.

*"Don’t chase. Don’t break formation. If you move, you move together."*

A pause as he checks the line forming around us.

*"If you get ordered to shift — you shift. No thinking."*

His eyes flick down the row once.

*"For now — just follow."*

The fort becomes controlled panic.

Orders shouted, corrected, repeated.

Horns overlapping until they stop meaning different things and just become pressure.

Groups spilling out of tents collide with forming lines, get redirected, get shoved into place.

Left flank.

We’re there.

Shoulder to shoulder.

Shields rising too early, then lowering, then rising again as someone yells at us to hold.

The treeline shifts.

At first it looks like wind.

Then it doesn’t.

Corbin walks the line once.

Not checking formation. Not correcting.

Just looking at faces he already knows might not be there later.

He stops at Darrin.

Then me.

A pause that feels longer than it is.

*"Stay alive."*

No weight added. No promise behind it.

Just instructions for something uncertain.

Someone down the line shouts.

Heads turn at once.

And the forest answers.

The Forest Hounds break from the treeline — not a trickle, not scouting movement.

A surge.

Low shapes moving fast across broken ground, spreading wide like something that already knows where the weak points are. Too many to count. Too coordinated to ignore.

The sound reaches us a second later — not growling, not howling.

Just impact in motion.

My heart hits my ribs hard enough to hurt.

My grip tightens on wood and leather until my knuckles go pale.

Every instinct says to step back.

To run.

To disappear into the line behind me.

‘Whatever it is… I’m going to survive this.’

The thought doesn’t feel brave.

It feels necessary.

Corbin’s voice cuts through one last time, low and steady.

*"Hold the shield."*

A pause.

*"Don’t let them break the edge."*

Another step as he adjusts his stance beside us.

*"You are not here to kill them."*

That makes the line tighten.

*"Don’t chase. Don’t break formation for kills. Your job is simple — push them inward."*

His eyes track the movement in the treeline.

*"We funnel them. Keep pressure on the flank so they turn toward the center."*

A distant crash echoes from somewhere beyond the walls — the first contact on another sector.

Corbin doesn’t look away.

*"If the edge collapses, they spill sideways. If they spill sideways, the center loses control."*

Silence tightens across the shield line.

He taps his shield once.

*"If someone goes down — the gap is filled immediately."*

A beat.

*"Not because they don’t matter."*

Another pause.

*"Because the shape matters more."*

That lands heavier than anything else.

Corbin exhales once.

*"Keep the funnel intact."*

Final glance down the line.

*"That’s all you are."*

The treeline shifts again.

Closer now.

No one speaks.

No one breathes properly.

Then someone down the line shouts.

And the Forest Hounds hit the field.
