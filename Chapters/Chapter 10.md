The ground changes before I see anything.

At first it isn’t something I recognize as danger. Just a strange shift under my boots — a deep, uneven tremor rolling through the earth like something moving fast beneath the forest line. Too heavy to ignore, but too far away to understand.

Then the sound arrives, and everything else disappears into it.

Not a roar. Not even separate noises anymore. Just impact layered over impact until it becomes one continuous pressure in the air, like the world itself is being struck again and again from the other side without pause.

The line tightens almost on instinct.

Not because anyone tells us to.

Because the body remembers before the mind catches up.

Somewhere behind and above us, a voice cuts through the noise — sharp, urgent, already strained by how much it has to hold together.

*“Left flank. Hold your line!”*

Another follows almost immediately, closer now, controlled but under pressure.

*“Spacing! Do not let them split the edge!”*

A horn answers from somewhere to the right, long and uneven, and another replies farther back, overlapping until direction stops meaning anything.

Everything is happening at once.

And I don’t understand any of it properly.

Only fragments reach me.

Only pressure stays consistent.

Corbin is near the line, but he isn’t the one giving orders. Higher up, just behind the second rank, I catch sight of another figure moving along the formation — the flank commander. He feels like he exists slightly above everything else, like the chaos has to pass through him before it becomes action.

His voice never rises, but it never gets lost either.

*“Second line ready.”*

*“Rotate on break.”*

*“Do not chase into the forest.”*

Each order is short, clipped, and followed immediately by movement somewhere in the line, as if the entire formation is already waiting for him to speak.

The treeline shifts.

At first it still looks uncertain — scattered dark shapes spilling out between the trunks in uneven waves, testing as they move, spreading instinctively toward whatever looks weakest.

Then they commit.

The Forest Hounds hit.

The impact lands somewhere to my left.

Not me.

Someone else.

A shield beside me jerks violently as a body slams into it from the front line, the force enough to push the man behind it half a step back, boots scraping stone as he fights to recover balance that is already gone.

*“Front— hold it—!”*

The line takes it.

Barely.

My breath catches late, like my body forgets how to do something as simple as breathing on time. My heart is pounding so hard I can hear it in my ears.

Another impact follows immediately, closer now, and the shock runs through the formation like a ripple through something too tightly stretched.

My stance shifts without me deciding it, just a fraction off, enough.

Corbin notices.

His hand snaps out and hits my shoulder, not hard enough to knock me down, but hard enough to fix me.

*“Foot back!”*

No anger. No patience either.

Just correction.

I obey instantly, stumbling slightly as I adjust.

*“Don’t lean in,”* he says, already looking away again.

*“Let the shield take it.”*

I nod too fast. I can’t help it.

Another wave hits the line, harder than the previous ones, and the entire formation shudders in place.

I try to match it, but everything feels slightly delayed now, like I’m always half a second behind what everyone else already understands.

Then a voice snaps somewhere nearby — not Corbin.

*“Kid— stop pushing!”*

I freeze.

My grip tightens until my knuckles burn under the leather.

I don’t even fully understand what I did wrong.

But I know I did something wrong.

The forest doesn’t stop.

It keeps pressing.

Closer now.

I try to follow the rhythm of the line, but there isn’t a rhythm I can see anymore. Only movement that keeps changing faster than I can adjust to.

Then the bodies in front of me shift.

The rotation begins.

Front line stepping back under pressure. Second line stepping forward to replace them. Everything happening in layers I can barely keep up with.

But nothing is clean here.

Everything is slightly off. Slightly delayed. Slightly too fast.

A gap opens for a moment where timing slips.

Not big.

But enough.

My hands are shaking. I don't remember when that started.

I react before I think and step forward into it—

Wrong direction.

Corbin grabs the back of my armor immediately and yanks me sideways out of it.

*“Not there!”*

I stumble back into position, my heart slamming so hard it feels like it’s competing with the noise around us.

The gap closes almost instantly.

Someone else is already filling it before I even fully register it existed.

The pressure never stops.

The hounds keep hitting the line without hesitation, without rhythm, without anything human in the way they keep testing us.

And somewhere inside all of it, I realize I can’t tell where the front line is anymore.

Only that everything is moving.

Only that if I stop moving correctly, even for a moment, something worse will happen.

I'm still shaking. My grip is too tight. My breath is too fast. I know I need to calm down — but knowing doesn't make it happen.

Another impact slams into the formation and the shield wall bends under it, not breaking, but straining in a way that feels temporary rather than stable.

A horn screams somewhere off to the side, high and fractured.

And the battle keeps going without waiting for me to catch up.

---

The line doesn’t break all at once.

At first, it holds.

Even with the constant impacts, even with the hounds probing the formation like they’re trying to find something soft inside steel and will, we stay upright. It stops feeling like a plan after a while. It becomes repetition. Shield up. Brace. Step when someone falls out of rhythm. Fill the space before it becomes a gap.

I lose track of how long it’s been early.

Minutes stop meaning anything.

Then longer than minutes.

At some point, I stop expecting anything to end at all.

There is no front anymore. Not in any way I can clearly point to. Only pressure pressing in from somewhere beyond the shields, constantly shifting, constantly testing.

My arms start to fail before I realize it properly.

At first it’s just fatigue. Something I can push through if I lock my stance and stop thinking about how heavy everything feels. But it keeps building anyway, creeping deeper until even holding the shield steady feels like I’m borrowing strength that isn’t mine.

The sounds around me stop turning into words I can understand.

They’re still shouting.

I think they are.

*“Hold!”*

*“Rotate!”*

*“Fill the gap!”*

But they don’t stay in my head long enough to matter.

They just pass through me.

At some point, the line shifts.

Not smoothly.

Something breaks on the left, and the entire formation reacts like it’s trying to repair itself while still under attack. Bodies move to cover, shields adjust, space closes and opens in places I don’t fully understand fast enough.

I try to follow it.

I really do.

But I’m behind it.

I step wrong without realizing it.

Then I correct.

Then I hesitate.

That hesitation is worse than the mistake.

Something slips through somewhere beyond my shield. I don’t even see it properly at first — only hear it, a sudden burst of noise behind me, steel scraping, someone shouting for a gap to be filled.

*“Fill it— now!”*

The line snaps back into place almost immediately.

Like nothing happened.

But I feel it anyway. That small instability that doesn’t go away anymore.

Another impact hits my shield.

Harder this time.

My arms take it, but something in the rhythm breaks. A second hit follows almost immediately after, then another, closer together now, no longer probing like before.

It’s not testing anymore.

It’s pushing.

My shield feels wrong in my hands.

Too heavy. Too slow.

I raise it late.

The angle isn’t right.

The force drives through me, pushing me back half a step without me deciding to move. I try to correct it, but the next impact comes before I can recover.

My grip starts slipping in small ways. Not fully losing it — just moments where it feels like my hands aren’t listening as quickly as they should.

Everything feels slightly off now. Like I’ve drifted a little out of sync with everyone else and can’t quite find my way back.

A voice cuts through behind me.

Closer than the others.

Corbin.

*“Eric— hold!”*

I hear it.

But it doesn’t land properly.

Another impact hits.

Square.

This time my arms don’t recover in time.

The shield jerks out of alignment.

I try to bring it back—

But I’m too slow.

For a split second, there’s nothing where there should be protection.

And something comes through.

There’s no clean moment of understanding. No clear thought that feels important enough to hold onto.

Just weight.

Then impact.

Everything folds backward at once, like my body forgot how to stay upright.

Sound disappears instantly.

Not fading.

Gone.

The shield slips from my hands.

And then there’s nothing.

---

Light seeps through the canvas in a dull, uneven wash that doesn’t feel like morning so much as exhaustion given shape.

For a while, I don’t understand where I am.

There is only noise.

Not the kind I remember from before. Not the shield line, not the forest, not the constant pressure of impact. This is different. Closer. Messier. Human in a way that feels worse.

Someone is screaming somewhere to my left. Not words I can catch. Just sound breaking apart before it can become meaning. Another voice answers, sharp and urgent, trying to push order into something that refuses to listen.

Footsteps cut through the space in fast, uneven bursts. Never stopping long enough to belong anywhere.

It takes me a moment to realize I’m lying down.

Longer to realize I didn’t choose to be.

My body feels like it has been taken apart and put back together without permission. My head aches in deep pulses that throb every time I try to focus. My ribs feel tight, like breathing is something I have to negotiate with. My right arm is so heavy I can barely tell where it ends.

I try to sit up.

The world tips immediately.

A wave of dizziness crashes through me, forcing me back down before I even understand what went wrong. I breathe through it, slow and uneven, until the room stops spinning and becomes something I can survive looking at.

Now I can see it properly.

A medical tent.

Rows of cots stretching in uneven lines. Some occupied. Some not. Some with people who look asleep in a way that doesn’t feel peaceful at all. Others are awake, staring at nothing, or at everything, like they haven’t decided which is worse yet.

People move between them constantly.

No one walks slowly.

No one wastes motion.

Bandages. Buckets. Blood-stained cloth. Orders shouted without stopping to see if they land. Everything is movement, everything is urgency, everything feels like it is continuing because stopping would mean something worse.

The smell hits me properly now that I’m aware enough to notice it.

Blood. Sweat. Wet cloth. Something sharp underneath it all, like crushed leaves trying and failing to cover iron.

Somewhere deeper in the tent, someone cries out. Not once. Not cleanly. In broken waves, like their body can’t decide how much pain it is allowed to feel at the same time.

This is war.

The thought doesn’t arrive clearly.

It settles.

My fingers twitch against the blanket. I notice bandages wrapped tightly around my forearm, my hand slightly numb inside them. My ribs are bound too, the pressure making every breath controlled and shallow, like I’m being forced to borrow air instead of owning it.

I stare at my hands for a while.

Trying to remember how I ended up here.

The last thing I remember is the shield.

Weight. Impact. Pressure without pause.

Then… nothing clean. No moment I can hold onto. Just a slipping feeling, like the world pulled away faster than I could follow.

A stretcher passes nearby. I hear it before I see it. Wood scraping. Fast footsteps. A voice trying to keep control without actually having any.

*“Move— move him through!”*

*“Bandage is slipping— hold it— hold it!”*

No one answers calmly.

No one has time for calm.

I try to sit up again, slower this time.

My body resists, but I push through it enough to lift myself halfway before stopping. The dizziness comes back, but weaker now, something I can lean against instead of being swallowed by.

From here, I can see more of the tent.

And more of what it is doing.

There are too many injured for it to feel temporary. Too many people being moved, cleaned, wrapped, ignored, reassigned. Some faces are still recognizable in a way that makes my stomach tighten, even if I can’t place where I know them from yet.

I don’t see Corbin.

I don’t see the flank commander.

I don’t see the line.

That thought lands late.

Heavy.

My throat feels dry when I try to speak, but nothing comes out properly. Just air, weak and useless.

Another scream rises somewhere behind me.

And I realize, my first battle ... has come to end.
