# The Grunt's Edge

## Core Premise

Eric was born in a fantasy world but was accidentally pulled into modern Earth at the age of six after a catastrophic spatial-temporal magical disaster.

For fourteen years, he lived an ordinary modern life.

At age twenty, the dimensional tether binding his soul to his original world finally snapped back, dragging him home.

Unfortunately, "home" is no longer home.

His parents are dead.

His younger sister barely remembers him.

His older brother spent fourteen years searching for a missing child who no longer exists.

Without identity papers, status, or family records, Eric enlists in the Royal Army under the alias "Vane" simply to obtain legal protection, food, shelter, and a stable income.

He believes military service will be an easy and secure life.

He is wrong.

---

## Core Themes

### Identity

Eric belongs to two worlds.

The modern world shaped most of his personality, morality, and worldview.

The fantasy world is where he was born.

He belongs to both.

He belongs to neither.

The story follows Eric's journey to discover who he truly is and where he belongs.

---

### Survival vs Conscience

Eric's greatest fear is death.

Unlike traditional heroes, he does not seek glory, power, or fame.

He wants safety.

He wants stability.

He wants to survive.

Yet despite constantly wanting to avoid trouble, he repeatedly finds himself helping others because he cannot ignore his conscience.

The conflict between self-preservation and compassion forms the core of his character.

---

### Systems Over Legends

The world worships heroes, noble mages, and famous warriors.

Reality is different.

Armies march because supplies arrive.

Cities survive because logistics function.

Campaigns are won through organization rather than individual glory.

Throughout the story, the importance of systems gradually surpasses the importance of legendary individuals.

---

### Finding Home

The story ultimately is not about saving the world.

It is about Eric building a life worth living.

A family.

A purpose.

A future.

By the end of the story, Eric finally chooses where he belongs rather than having fate choose for him.

---

## Story Direction

The world gradually descends into greater chaos as monster populations increase and military demand rises.

Eric remains a small figure in the background of history.

He is rarely the hero of the story.

Instead, he repeatedly influences important events through small actions, practical decisions, and moments of unexpected courage.

While heroes gain recognition, Eric quietly becomes someone soldiers trust when survival matters.

The story concludes with Eric finally understanding his identity, establishing a family, and stepping into a future of his own choosing.

The ending is not the conclusion of his life.

It is the beginning of the next chapter.
