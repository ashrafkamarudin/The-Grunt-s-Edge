Synopsis

A 20-year-old man named Eric, who was pulled from a fantasy world to Earth at age six and spent fourteen years as a disconnected outsider, is suddenly pulled back to his original world. He wakes naked in a forest, stumbles to a frontier village called Eastwood, and recovers under the care of a talkative girl named Lena.

Days later, a conscription order arrives. The village is too poor to pay the levy. The elder, Barrow, makes Eric a cold deal — volunteer for the army, and they'll claim he's from Eastwood. No questions about his past. Eric accepts not out of cour age but resignation.

He marches with other conscripts to a garrison city called Grayford. After two weeks of brutal training, he'll be sent to Fort Lowen on the north-east frontier, where monster activity is escalating. Deep in the forest lies an ancient elven ruin, newly exposed, leaking mana that draws monsters — and the attention of both the Empire and the elven kingdom of the Sylvan Reach.

Eric is not the hero of this story. He's an observer — a Tier 1 soldier who will never reach the heights of the heroes he hears about in camp rumors and tavern talk. The story follows his survival, his quiet observations, and the slow realization that the systems running the war matter more than any legendary individual.
