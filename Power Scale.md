# ⚔️ POWER STRUCTURE — The Grunt’s Edge World

Think of power in your world as layers of battlefield impact, not just “strong vs weak.”

---

# 🪖 TIER 0 — CIVILIANS (NON-COMBATANTS)

## Who they are
- Farmers, merchants, craftsmen, porters  
- No combat training  
- No access to magic systems  

## Survival reality
- Completely dependent on military protection  
- Vulnerable to monsters, raids, famine  

## Narrative role
> The people Eric is ultimately trying to protect (even if he doesn’t admit it)

---

# 🪓 TIER 1 — FOOT SOLDIERS (ERIC’S LEVEL)

## Who they are
- Infantry, spearmen, shield units  
- Basic trained recruits  
- Some veterans  

## Capability
- Can kill monsters only in groups  
- Rely on formation > individual skill  
- Survival depends on discipline, not talent  

## Reality check
- Most deaths happen here  
- Individual strength barely matters  

## Strength definition
> “Can you survive a battle line collapse?”

Eric sits here.

Even with his pocket ability:

- he is NOT a powerhouse  
- he is a high-survivability anomaly inside Tier 1  

---

# 🧱 TIER 2 — ELITE SOLDIERS / SPECIALISTS

## Who they are
- Veteran infantry  
- Monster hunters  
- Knight trainees (non-noble or low noble)  
- Squad leaders  

## Capability
- Can handle monsters solo in favorable conditions  
- Fight in small groups instead of full formations  
- Tactical awareness > raw strength  

## Role in war
- Turn chaotic fights into manageable ones  
- Anchor battlefield stability  

## Important detail
> This is where “named soldiers” begin to exist

Eric will brush against this tier over time.

---

# 🛡️ TIER 3 — HERO-CLASS INDIVIDUALS (PUBLIC “HEROES”)

## Who they are
- Noble knights  
- Famous adventurers  
- Sponsored combat mages  
- “Hero” branded individuals  

## Capability
- Can wipe squads alone  
- Can turn battlefield sections  
- Highly survivable (with support)  

## But important limitation:
> They are NOT independent war winners.

They are:

- force multipliers  
- not armies  

Without logistics + infantry support:

- they die or retreat  

## Social perception vs reality

### Public view:
> Heroes win wars

### Military reality:
> Heroes survive battles long enough for armies to win wars

---

# 🔥 TIER 4 — BATTLEFIELD MAGES (ARTILLERY CLASS)

## Who they are
- Licensed mages  
- Noble-trained spellcasters  
- Military contracted magic units  

## Capability
- Large-scale destruction (fire, ice, wind, spatial bursts)  
- Area denial  
- Monster clearing  

## Weakness
- Extremely fragile  
- Require protection while casting  
- High mana/resource dependency  

## Battlefield role
> “Stationary weapons that move slowly”

They are not fighters.

They are assets.

---

# 🏰 TIER 5 — COMMAND-LEVEL INDIVIDUALS

## Who they are
- Generals  
- High nobles  
- Arch-mages  
- Strategic commanders  

## Capability
- Do NOT fight directly  
- Control outcomes via:
  - troop movement  
  - supply chains  
  - battlefield positioning  
  - deployment of heroes/mages  

## Key truth:
> They decide who wins before the battle even starts

This is where Benson will eventually sit.

---

# 🧠 TIER 6 — SYSTEM DOMINANCE (THE REAL POWER)

This is your secret layer.

Not a person.

A concept.

---

## Includes:
- logistics networks (Benson’s domain)  
- supply chains  
- recruitment systems  
- military doctrine  
- intelligence networks  

---

## Truth of your world:

Armies do not win because of heroes.  
They win because systems don’t collapse.

If systems break:

- heroes die  
- mages run out of mana supplies  
- infantry collapses
