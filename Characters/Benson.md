# Benson

## Basic Information

* Name: Benson
* Role: Eldest sibling / family caretaker / future administrative worker
* Social status: Commoner
* Background: Extremely poor rural family

---

## Background

Benson grew up in a poor commoner household with his younger siblings Eric and Elysia.

When Eric disappeared at age 6, the family collapsed emotionally and financially.

Their parents eventually died due to prolonged hardship and instability.

At a young age, Benson became the de facto head of the family.

He took responsibility for survival, food, shelter, and protection of his remaining sister.

---

## Personality

Benson is ordinary in every sense.

He is not gifted, not ambitious in a grand way, and not naturally talented.

However, he is:

* Responsible
* Steady under pressure
* Hardworking
* Deeply family-oriented
* Quietly persistent

He expresses love through action rather than words.

---

## Skills Development

Originally illiterate, Benson later taught himself reading and writing to improve his job prospects.

He slowly transitioned into administrative work and basic clerical roles in order to secure stable income.

He believes systems and bureaucracy are the most reliable tools for survival in an unstable world.

---

## Motivation

Benson’s life revolves around two core goals:

1. Keep Elysia alive and give her a better future
2. Find Eric, or at least uncover what happened to him

Even as life forces him into bureaucracy and logistics work, he never stops quietly searching for traces of his lost brother.

---

## Flaws

* Overworks himself without noticing personal limits
* Believes hard work alone can fix systemic problems
* Tends to reduce emotional issues into “tasks to solve”
* Struggles to fully process grief, instead converting it into responsibility

---

## Narrative Role

Benson represents the “system-level survival path” of a commoner.

While others seek glory or power, Benson works within institutions to stabilize life for those he loves.

Unknowingly, his administrative work begins to connect him to the military systems that will eventually intersect with Eric’s life.

---

## Relationship to Eric

Eric’s disappearance defines much of Benson’s life.

Even without certainty of survival, Benson continues acting as if Eric can still be found.

This belief becomes both his motivation and emotional burden.

---

## Relationship to Elysia

Benson recognizes Elysia’s magical talent early and invests in her future despite their poverty.

He sees her as the family’s best chance of escaping poverty.

At the same time, he fears losing her to a world he cannot follow.
