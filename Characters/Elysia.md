# Elysia

## Basic Information

* Name: Elysia
* Role: Younger sister / magic user / future academy mage
* Social status: Commoner
* Talent level: Above average magical ability (not elite noble tier)

---

## Background

Elysia grew up in extreme poverty after the disappearance of her older brother Eric and the eventual death of her parents.

Her upbringing is defined by scarcity, instability, and survival.

Unlike most commoner children, she showed early signs of magical aptitude.

Recognizing this, Benson supported her development as a potential path out of poverty.

---

## Personality

Elysia is:

* Focused
* Determined
* Quietly ambitious
* Emotionally controlled
* Highly observant
* Practical about survival

She is not arrogant about her talent.

Instead, she is aware that talent alone is not enough without resources or access.

---

## Magic Specialization

Elysia’s magical growth is shaped by necessity rather than luxury.

Her focus tends toward:

* Detection magic
* Tracking
* Spatial sensing
* Environmental anomaly awareness

Her training is limited by lack of funding, forcing her to develop efficiency over raw power.

---

## Motivation

Elysia is driven by one core belief:

> Magic is the only realistic way out of poverty.

Her goals are:

1. Improve her family’s living conditions
2. Gain access to structured magical education
3. Achieve financial stability through magical employment or sponsorship

Unspoken beneath all of this is her desire to understand what happened to Eric.

---

## Flaws

* Overly serious at a young age
* Ties self-worth to achievement and output
* Can unintentionally reduce relationships into goals or outcomes
* Limited exposure to emotional processing outside survival context

---

## Narrative Role

Elysia represents upward mobility through talent in a rigid class system.

Her presence bridges the gap between:

* Commoner life
* Military systems
* Elite magical institutions

Her tracking abilities eventually make her sensitive to spatial anomalies connected to forbidden or unstable magic.

---

## Relationship to Eric

Elysia barely remembers Eric, but grows up shaped by his absence.

He becomes an emotional anchor point in her identity, even if vague and incomplete.

---

## Relationship to Benson

Elysia respects Benson’s sacrifices and efforts.

She also feels the weight of being the “hope of the family,” which creates internal pressure to succeed.

Benson’s support becomes both her foundation and responsibility.
