# World Building

## General Tone

A grounded military fantasy focused on survival, logistics, infantry life, and the realities of warfare.

The setting emphasizes mud, exhaustion, supply shortages, military bureaucracy, and the experiences of ordinary soldiers rather than legendary heroes.

---

## The Monster Crisis

Monsters are a permanent threat to civilization.

In recent decades, monster populations have begun increasing at an alarming rate.

Trade routes become increasingly dangerous.

Border settlements disappear.

Military casualties continue rising.

The central challenge of the age is not defeating a singular evil but surviving a world that is becoming progressively more hostile.

---

## Technology Level

The world resembles a late medieval society.

Common transportation includes:

* Horses
* Oxen
* Wagons
* River transport

Advanced military organizations and wealthy elites often employ domesticated monsters for transport and warfare.

---

## Military Structure

The Royal Kingdom relies heavily on:

* Infantry formations
* Spears and pikes
* Defensive tactics
* Logistics and organization

Because powerful mages are rare and expensive, common soldiers remain essential to warfare.

Military service provides:

* Legal identity
* Food
* Shelter
* Income
* Social mobility

For many commoners, the army is the only path to a stable life.

---

## Magic

Magic is the dominant supernatural force.

However, magic remains limited.

No individual possesses world-ending power.

Even elite mages require support structures, resources, and protection.

Battlefield mages function similarly to artillery units.

They are powerful but vulnerable.

---

## Social Structure

Society is heavily stratified.

### Commoners

* Majority population
* Limited education
* Little political influence

### Nobility

* Control wealth and land
* Maintain military power
* Dominate magical institutions

### Mages

High-level magic users are heavily regulated.

Certain magical disciplines are restricted.

Unauthorized use can result in imprisonment, execution, or forced military service.

---

## Hero Culture

Heroes are celebrated by society and viewed as symbols of power and survival.

Ordinary soldiers often **revere them**, because heroes have a significantly higher chance of surviving battles and turning the tide of conflicts. In a world where commoners are accustomed to being disregarded by higher ranks, this survival advantage makes heroes invaluable despite their flaws.

However, many heroes are also arrogant or reckless, acting without concern for formations or the safety of those beneath them. This creates tension on the battlefield.

Ordinary soldiers tolerate and even respect this behavior, because survival takes priority over pride. Even if heroes are difficult to work with, being near them often increases a soldier’s chance of coming home alive.

This creates a cultural contradiction: heroes are both admired and resented, but ultimately depended upon.

---

## Major Factions

### Royal Kingdom

Disciplined military power emphasizing organization and logistics.

### High Hegemony

Mage-dominated empire ruled by powerful magical bloodlines.

### Neutral Guilds

Independent organizations controlling trade, adventuring services, and private security.
