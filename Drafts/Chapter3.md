# Chapter 3 — Outline

## Part 1: Waking
- Eric drifts in and out. Voices arguing somewhere distant — he's too weak to follow.
- Wakes fully in a small room. Low ceiling. Fire crackling. Smell of stew.
- Knee wrapped in cloth. Palms cleaned. Foot bandaged.
- A girl is there. She sees he's awake. She starts talking.
- He doesn't respond.

## Part 2: Two Days of Silence
- She talks constantly. He listens.
- Mundane rambling:
  - Her neighbour's goat got loose again
  - The kids in the village are too noisy
  - Her cooking came out well today, does he want more?
  - She stubbed her toe earlier
  - She thinks the blacksmith's son is an idiot
- Eric responds with nods, shakes, grunts.
- He's catching words. More each hour. The language is coming back.
- The father comes and goes. Drops meat on the table. Eats in silence.

## Part 3: The Question
- Second or third day. A quiet moment.
- She stops mid-sentence. Looks at him differently.
- *"sigh... who are you?"*
- Pause.
- His first words in fourteen years:
- *"I am... Eric."*

## Part 4: Aftermath
- She stares. Then accepts it. Goes back to rambling.
- The world is still alien, but he's no longer alone.
